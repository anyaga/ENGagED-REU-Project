Abstract

Leadership has a been a term that is usually misconstrued. While many see leadership as the ability to direct teams to deadlines or goals in an appropriate time span, more goes into making a good leader. True leadership is the ability to help other reach their full potential. While this could tie into reaching goals and deadlines, leadership isn’t bound to this. True leadership involved personal growth (---find quote that discuss this type of leadership----). Engineering is a field that has struggled heavily with portraying true leadership. With a focus on creation of a product or meeting particular deadlines, engineers often fail to foster a community in which all members of a team can succeed. (----find quote supporting this ------) Given the racial disparities within the engineering field, this failure in engineering leadership has even greater effects on marginalized groups. (---add statistic on engineering leadership for marginalized groups--).

Given this status, marginalized engineer must take on particular types of leadership to help themselves and individuals with similar identities to succeed. (---methods---) (--data analysis--). Through the analysis of several interviews using the multidimensional model of racial identity and critical race theory, three leadership archetypes where established that would best help marginalized groups succeed in engineering. These archetypes included the advocate leader, the self-preserving leader, and the “non-traditional” leader. These forms of leadership create environments in which marginalized individual and groups can succeed.
